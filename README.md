# Can They Afford It?

A companion piece to [isaiprofitable.com](https://isaiprofitable.com/).

That site asks whether AI is profitable. It isn't — for anyone. This one asks the
question that actually decides who's still standing in 2029: **can they afford to
keep losing?**

### Burn Coverage = operating cash flow ÷ capital expenditure

- `> 1.0×` — the core business funds the entire AI buildout and still throws off cash (*house money*)
- `< 1.0×` — spending more than the whole company earns, funding the gap with debt or outside capital (*credit card*)
- *no fortress* — no independent operating cash flow at all; the spend runs entirely on investors' money

Flip the page's **FY2025 ↔ 2026-guidance** toggle and watch every fortress slide
below 1.0×: on next year's spending plans, *none of the seven cover their own capex.*
The hyperscalers aren't a different species from Oracle — they're 12–18 months
behind it on the same slope, just with net cash instead of net debt.

### Notes

Figures are latest fiscal year as of June 2026. Reported numbers are sourced to
filings; private-company figures (OpenAI, Anthropic) are press estimates, flagged
amber in the footer. "Capex" is used as the AI-spend proxy because for these firms
it is now overwhelmingly AI data-centre buildout. Not investment advice; just arithmetic.

Single static `index.html`, no dependencies, no build step.
