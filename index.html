AI IS UNPROFITABLE FOR ALL OF THEM. Three can shrug it off — Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta fund the entire buildout from spare cash.  One fortress is straining — Amazon’s free cash flow has nearly vanished.  One is doing it on a credit card — Oracle spends ~2× what it earns, on $129B of debt.  Two are spending other people’s money — OpenAI and Anthropic have no business underneath at all.
Methodology — Burn Coverage = operating cash flow ÷ capital expenditure. Above 1.0×: the core business pays for the whole AI buildout and still throws off cash — house money. Below 1.0×: spending more than the entire company generates, funding the gap with debt or outside capital — credit card. No fortress: no independent operating cash flow whatsoever — the spend runs entirely on investors’ capital. Flip the toggle to 2026 guidance and watch even the fortresses slide toward the line.

How to read it

A tall green bar dwarfing a short red one = a cash fortress easily covering its AI spend. When red overtakes green, the company is funding AI from its balance sheet, not its surplus. The labs have no green bar at all. Net cash is positive (own the money) or negative (owe it).

Methodology & honest caveats

Burn Coverage uses reported operating cash flow ÷ reported capital expenditure. “Capex” is used as the AI-spend proxy because for these firms it is now overwhelmingly AI data-centre buildout — though a minority is non-AI (fulfilment, offices). Microsoft capex shown on the cash “additions to property & equipment” basis (~$64B FY25; ~$88B incl. finance leases). Amazon OCF is trailing-twelve-months to Mar 2026; net cash shown on a long-term-debt basis (it flips to net-debt if operating leases are included). Net cash = cash & marketable securities − debt; Oracle uses total notes payable ($129.5B). The labs’ “burn” is annualised compute spend, an estimate; they have no public cash-flow statement. The $850B combined-capex ticker is an estimate built from 2026 guidance midpoints and is illustrative.
