The question isaiprofitable.com forgot to ask. AI is unprofitable for every one of them — that part’s settled. The only question that decides who’s still standing in 2029 is who can afford to keep losing. Figures: latest fiscal year, June 2026.

AI IS UNPROFITABLE FOR ALL OF THEM. Three can shrug it off — Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta fund the entire buildout from spare cash.  One fortress is straining — Amazon’s free cash flow has nearly vanished.  One is doing it on a credit card — Oracle spends ~2× what it earns, on ~$156B of debt.  Two are spending other people’s money — OpenAI and Anthropic have no business underneath at all.
Methodology — Burn Coverage = operating cash flow ÷ capital expenditure. Above 1.0×: the core business pays for the whole AI buildout and still throws off cash — house money. Below 1.0×: spending more than the entire company generates, funding the gap with debt or outside capital — credit card. No fortress: no independent operating cash flow whatsoever — the spend runs entirely on investors’ capital. Flip the toggle to 2026 guidance and watch even the fortresses slide toward the line.

How to read it

A tall green bar dwarfing a short red one = a cash fortress easily covering its AI spend. When red overtakes green, the company is funding AI from its balance sheet, not its surplus. The labs have no green bar at all. Net cash is positive (own the money) or negative (owe it). Two sort lenses disagree at the top: Most precarious blends self-funding with cash buffer (worst first); Coverage is the raw OCF÷capex ratio (best first). And the keystone fact — $300B of Oracle’s $638B backlog (~47%) is a single OpenAI deal — means the most precarious public name’s biggest asset is an IOU from the most precarious private one. Affordability all the way down.

Methodology & honest caveats

Burn Coverage uses reported operating cash flow ÷ reported capital expenditure. “Capex” is used as the AI-spend proxy because for these firms it is now overwhelmingly AI data-centre buildout — though a minority is non-AI (fulfilment, offices). Microsoft capex shown on the cash “additions to property & equipment” basis (~$64B FY25; ~$88B incl. finance leases). Amazon OCF is trailing-twelve-months to Mar 2026; net cash shown on a long-term-debt basis (it flips to net-debt if operating leases are included). Net cash = cash & marketable securities − debt; Oracle shown on the ~$156B total-debt figure now cited (up ~50% YoY; the Q4 release line was $129.5B notes payable) less ~$11B cash ≈ −$145B. The labs own no data centres, so their bar is annualised compute commitment — OpenAI ~$1.4T ÷ ~8yr ≈ $175B, Anthropic ~$200B ÷ 5yr ≈ $40B — an estimate, not capex; they have no public cash-flow statement. The $950B combined-capex ticker is an estimate built from 2026 guidance midpoints (labs on a commitment basis) and is illustrative.
